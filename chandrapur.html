About Chandrapur

Known as the "Black Gold City" for its coal deposits and "Geological Museum" for its rich mineral diversity, Chandrapur is a major industrial power center. It is also home to the famous Tadoba-Andhari Tiger Reserve. The Morva Airport facilitates connectivity for VIPs and corporates visiting these key industrial and tourism sites.

Chandrapur Morva Airport. Chandrapur lies on terrain rich in coal deposits, hence known as Black Gold City and Geological Museum. This city is a center for coal mining, cement making, paper manufacturing, and ferro alloy manufacturing. It is a major power generation center of the state. Apart from being an education hub, this district has the Tadoba-Andhari Tiger reserve, having the maximum number of tigers population in the state. The existing Morva Airport has an airstrip of 950 meters length. The airport is primarily used for non-scheduled flights of VIPs and corporates.
