Power

The Government of India, Ministry of Commerce, vide Notification dated 03 Mar 2010, changed the status of the MIHAN-SEZ Developers as Deemed Distribution Licensee.

In pursuance of the above, MADC approached the Hon'ble Maharashtra Energy Regulatory Commission (MERC).

On 3 Dec 2013, the MERC notified, the MERC (Specific conditions of Distribution Licensee applicable to Maharashtra Airport Development Company Limited for MIHAN SEZ) regulations, 2013 which inter-alia recognizes that MADC's license shall remain valid for a period of 25 years with effect from 29 May 2007 or unless such license is revoked, whichever is earlier.

Generation (Power Plant)

M/s Abhijeet is awarded the project contract through competitive bidding and commissioned the (4X61.5) 246 MW generating plant to supply uninterrupted and quality power supply to the MIHAN SEZ consumers.

DG sets having capacity of 25 MW have been commissioned as a back-up.

Transmission

To supply power to the MIHAN consumers, a 220/33 KV receiving station is set up with transformation capacity of 82.5X3 MVA that is 247.5 MVA.

11 KM 220 KV DC transmission line from the generating station to the receiving station is in operation.

Distribution (Consumer Base)

Nagpur is a favored destination of IT/ITES, BPO operations. There are 63 IT companies operating in Nagpur. Also the textile, garments, food processing, Health Industry, pharmaceuticals and Bio- medical Industries etc. are the consumers of MADC MIHAN.

The estimated load forecast for the five years controlling period in MIHAN SEZ area is @ 94 MW.

The power requirement of MIHAN SEZ would be around 200 MW by 2025.

The distribution Network consists of 4 PSS i.e. 33/11 KV substations with total transformation capacity of 160 MW and 2no DTH to feed power to the consumers.

A ring main at 11 KV network is formed to have an alternate supply arrangement to the consumers so that there will be minimum interruptions to the consumers. The 33KV network of 44 KM & 11KV network of 62 KM is established by laying underground cable.

The EHV network will be developed as per the requirement of the consumers for which spare bays are available at 220/33 KV receiving S/s.

Status

At present the existing load of the consumers in MIHAN SEZ is to the tune of 2 MW to 5 MW for which the power is being purchased through short Term power bidding procedure set up by Ministry of Power, Government of India so that the consumer shall get cheaper power.

Presently the power is purchased through power exchange on daily basis.

A office and the complaint centre is established for the services to be provided to the consumers of MIHAN SEZ at central facility building at Khapri, Nagpur under the control of Chief Engineer, MADC, Nagpur.
